    # About the typefaces

    Sentinel digital has only two type faces; Poppins and Meta Serif Pro. These two typefaces compliment each other well and have distinct use cases.

    # Meta Serif Pro

    Meta serif pro works well as header and body text for actual content such as articles, testimonies and editorials. This is due to the fact that it is traditionally used in newspaper and magazines. This provides readers with a sense of familiarity when reading content on the site. It's high x-height, even stroke width and modest serifs makes it's letters appear smooth and elegant which in turn makes reading effortless.

    # Poppins

    Poppins a fresh and clean sans serif font and lends itself well to a user interface environment due to its mono-linear (all around even stroke width) construction. Its easy to calculate proper spacing for this typeface. One could easily make the character spacing match up with margins and padding of its surrounding. This typeface also works well for non-critical content such as ads, user prompts and legal language.

    # Typescale

    All typography in this design system comes in multiples of 4 with 12 pixels being the smallest font size and 40 pixels being the largest. We use multiples of 4 because typography generally aligns well with a 4-grid baseline on screens. Line height must also be divisible by 4 in order to keep to the baseline. This makes it easy to scale a piece of text up or down. Apple and Google are examples of mature organizations who use this scale system for their design systems and its reflected in all their products.

    Most browsers set their default font size at 16 pixels which makes 16 the perfect minimum font size to use for body text in this design system.

    # Headers

    Its large size sets it apart as a main header on any page and calls the users attention as the entry point for reading.

    This is the font style that would typically be used for article headings and titles as well as part of the main header on any page. It helps to capture the users attention and serves as an entry point for reading.
